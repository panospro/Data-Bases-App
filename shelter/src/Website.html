Welcome to Our Animal Shelter!

Our animal shelter was founded just a few short years ago by a group of compassionate animal lovers who saw the need for a safe and loving home for abandoned and homeless pets in our community. We started small, with a handful of dedicated volunteers and a single building to house and care for a few dozen animals. But from the very beginning, we were driven by our deep commitment to improving the lives of animals and promoting the human-animal bond.

As word of our mission and dedication spread, we began to grow. More and more people joined our team of volunteers, and we were able to expand our services and reach to help more animals in need. We added new buildings and programs, and our small shelter began to make a big impact.

Today, we are proud to be a thriving and vital part of the community, providing care and support to hundreds of animals and their families each year. We are always looking for new ways to make a difference and to reach even more animals in need. We are grateful for the support of our donors, volunteers, and community partners, who make our work possible and help us to move forward in our mission.

At our animal shelter, we are dedicated to rescuing and rehoming abandoned and homeless pets. We provide a safe and loving environment for them until they find their forever homes.

Our team of passionate and dedicated staff and volunteers work tirelessly to care for the animals and find them loving homes. We also offer a range of services to the community, including adoptions, spay/neuter surgeries, and microchipping.

We believe that every animal deserves a chance at a happy and healthy life, and we are committed to making a difference in the lives of as many animals as possible. We hope you will consider supporting us in our mission by adopting, volunteering, or donating to our shelter. Thank you for your support!

Volunteer Jobs

Our volunteers are an integral part of our team and play a vital role in the care and well-being of the animals at our shelter. Some of the tasks and responsibilities that volunteers may be involved in include:

  • Socializing with the animals: Spending time with the animals, playing with them, and providing them with love and attention is an important part of their care and helps to improve their mental and emotional health. Volunteers may also be involved in basic training and obedience exercises with the animals.
  • Cleaning cages and kennels: Maintaining a clean and healthy environment for the animals is essential to their well-being. Volunteers may be responsible for cleaning and disinfecting cages, kennels, and other areas of the shelter, as well as changing bedding and providing fresh water and food for the animals.
  • Grooming pets: Many of the animals at our shelter have long fur or require regular grooming to maintain their health and appearance. Volunteers may be responsible for brushing, bathing, and drying the animals, as well as trimming nails and cleaning ears.
  • Helping with events: Our shelter hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including adoption days, fundraising events, and community outreach events. Volunteers may be asked to help with set-up, registration, and other tasks to ensure the smooth running of these events.

Frequently Asked Questions:

How can I adopt a pet from your shelter?

To adopt a pet from our shelter, you will need to fill out an adoption application and meet with one of our adoption counselors. For more information, please visit our adoption page.

Do you accept donations?

Yes, we greatly appreciate donations to help us care for the animals at our shelter. You can make a donation online through our donation page, or you can drop off donations at the shelter during our hours of operation.

What types of animals do you have available for adoption?

At this very moment we only have dogs available for adoption, in the future we will try to include cats, rabbits, and small mammals such as guinea pigs and hamsters. We may also inlcude reptiles and birds. Please check our adoption page for a current list of available animals.

I lost my pet. How can I find out if it's at your shelter?

If you have lost your pet, we recommend checking our lost and found page to see if it has been brought to our shelter. You can also call us at 2310-156-156 to ask if your pet has been found and brought to the shelter. We recommend visiting the shelter in person to look for your pet as well.

I'm interested in volunteering at your shelter. How do I get started?

We are always in need of volunteers to help care for the animals at our shelter. To get started, please visit our volunteering page and view the details of volunteering, then fill out the volunteer application, located in the bottom of this page. We will then contact you to schedule an orientation and training session.

Do you offer low-cost or free spay/neuter services?

Yes, we offer low-cost spay/neuter services to the public to help reduce pet overpopulation. For more information on pricing and how to schedule an appointment, you call, email, direct message us in our social media .

Do you have a waiting list for adoptions?

We do not have a waiting list for adoptions, but the availability of specific animals can vary. We recommend checking our adoption page regularly or calling the shelter at 2310-156-156 to inquire about specific animals you are interested in adopting.
